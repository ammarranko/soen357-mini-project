Introduction & Problem

Define the problem space and what success looks like.

Context

People managing chronic conditions often use fragmented tools (alarms, notes, memory). This creates missed doses, confusion, and stress around appointments and communication.

Replace this with your exact context + target population.

Problem statement

Users struggle to consistently remember medications, track adherence, manage appointments, and communicate with healthcare professionals in one place.

Design goal: Create an interaction flow that reduces cognitive load, improves adherence, and feels trustworthy and calm.

Research

Show evidence: method, participants, insights, and design implications.

Method

  • Survey: 10+ participants
  • Optional: 1–2 short interviews
  • Questions focused on routines, pain points, priorities

Key findings

  • Many users forget at least once per week
  • Phone alarms are common but incomplete
  • Logging must be fast (≤ 2 taps)
  • Older users need clarity + large text

Design implications

  • One-tap “Taken” from reminder
  • Persistent reminders until confirmed
  • Simple weekly adherence summary
  • Accessibility-first typography & contrast

Accessibility-focused

Goals
